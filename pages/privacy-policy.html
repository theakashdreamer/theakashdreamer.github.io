Privacy Policy

Super EMI Key / LockSystem Device Management Platform

Effective Date: 26 April 2026
Last Updated: 26 April 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how Super EMI Key / LockSystem collects, uses, stores, and protects information when customers, retailers, administrators, or managed Android devices use our device management services.

1. Introduction

Super EMI Key / LockSystem is an Android Enterprise-based device management solution designed to help businesses, retailers, and organizations manage Android devices remotely for legitimate business and security purposes.

Our platform may be used for device enrollment, policy management, remote lock, kiosk mode, lost mode, device tracking, app installation, and other enterprise device control features.

By using our application, admin dashboard, backend services, or enrolled devices, you agree to the practices described in this Privacy Policy.

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Account and Organization Information

When an organization or retailer uses our platform, we may collect:

  • Organization name
  • Admin or retailer name
  • Email address
  • Mobile number
  • Business details
  • Login credentials or authentication identifiers

2.2 Device Information

When a device is enrolled or registered, we may collect:

  • Device ID or enrollment identifier
  • Android version
  • Device brand, model, and manufacturer
  • Serial number, where permitted by Android Enterprise APIs
  • IMEI or hardware identifiers, only where legally permitted and technically available
  • App package name and app version
  • Device owner or profile owner status
  • Policy status and compliance status
  • Last online time and sync status

2.3 Device Management Data

To provide remote management features, we may process:

  • Device lock or unlock status
  • Kiosk mode status
  • Lost mode message
  • Policy configuration
  • Installed managed applications
  • Command history such as lock, wipe, reboot, or policy update commands
  • Enrollment token and QR provisioning status

2.4 Location Information

If enabled by the organization and permitted by the device/user, we may collect location information for security, recovery, attendance, compliance, or device tracking purposes.

Location collection depends on device permissions, Android version, management mode, and organization policy.

2.5 Firebase and Notification Data

We may use Firebase Cloud Messaging or similar services to send device commands and notifications. For this purpose, we may collect:

  • FCM registration token
  • Notification delivery status
  • Command payload status
  • Device connectivity status

3. How We Use Information

We use collected information for the following purposes:

  • To enroll Android devices into enterprise management
  • To create and apply device policies
  • To remotely lock or unlock managed devices
  • To enable kiosk mode or dedicated device mode
  • To send lost mode messages
  • To install or manage approved business applications
  • To monitor device compliance and security status
  • To prevent unauthorized device usage
  • To provide admin dashboard functionality
  • To send alerts, notifications, and command updates
  • To improve platform security and reliability
  • To comply with legal, contractual, or enterprise requirements

4. Android Enterprise and Android Management API

Our platform may use Google Android Management API, Android Device Policy, Managed Google Play, Firebase, and related Google services to manage enrolled Android devices.

When a device is enrolled through QR code, zero-touch enrollment, or enterprise provisioning, Android Device Policy may be installed and used to apply organization-defined policies.

Depending on the management mode, the organization may be able to perform actions such as:

  • Apply security restrictions
  • Install approved apps
  • Block factory reset or safe boot where supported
  • Lock the device
  • Wipe the device in permitted scenarios
  • Restrict system settings
  • Enable kiosk mode
  • Track device compliance
Device management features are intended only for legally owned, authorized, or organization-managed devices. Unauthorized monitoring or control of personal devices is strictly prohibited.

5. Legal Basis and Consent

We process information based on one or more of the following legal bases:

  • User or organization consent
  • Business contract or service agreement
  • Legitimate security and device management interest
  • Compliance with applicable laws
  • Protection against fraud, misuse, or unauthorized access

For managed devices, the organization or device owner is responsible for informing end users about device management policies before enrollment.

6. Data Sharing

We do not sell personal data. We may share limited information only in the following cases:

  • With authorized organization administrators
  • With retailers or business partners responsible for device management
  • With cloud service providers used for hosting, database, notifications, or analytics
  • With Google services such as Android Management API, Firebase, and Managed Google Play
  • When required by law, court order, or government authority
  • To protect our rights, users, systems, or business security

7. Data Storage and Security

We use reasonable technical and organizational security measures to protect collected information from unauthorized access, misuse, loss, or disclosure.

Security measures may include:

  • Encrypted communication using HTTPS
  • Secure authentication
  • Role-based admin access
  • Restricted database access
  • Audit logs for sensitive commands
  • Secure cloud infrastructure
  • Periodic security review

However, no internet-based system can be guaranteed to be completely secure. Users and organizations are also responsible for protecting their login credentials and admin access.

8. Remote Lock, Wipe, and Device Control

Our system may allow authorized administrators to send commands to managed devices. These commands may include:

  • Lock device
  • Unlock device where supported
  • Enable or disable kiosk mode
  • Display lost mode message
  • Update device policy
  • Wipe device where permitted by policy and law
  • Install or remove managed applications

These actions are available only to authorized administrators and should be used only for legitimate business, EMI security, lost device recovery, or enterprise compliance purposes.

9. Children’s Privacy

Our services are not intended for direct use by children without proper consent from a parent, guardian, school, or authorized organization.

If the platform is used for student, child, or dependent device management, the responsible organization or guardian must ensure that proper consent and legal permissions are obtained.

10. Data Retention

We retain collected information only for as long as necessary to provide our services, comply with legal obligations, resolve disputes, enforce agreements, and maintain business records.

Device data may be deleted or anonymized after device unenrollment, contract termination, or account deletion, subject to legal and operational requirements.

Depending on applicable laws, users or organizations may have rights to:

  • Request access to stored information
  • Request correction of inaccurate data
  • Request deletion of data
  • Withdraw consent where applicable
  • Request device unenrollment, subject to organization policy
  • Raise privacy or security concerns

Requests can be submitted using the contact details provided below.

Device unenrollment may be controlled by the organization, retailer, or authorized administrator depending on the business agreement and device ownership model.

For data deletion requests, users or organizations may contact us. We will verify the request and process it according to applicable laws and business obligations.

Our platform may use third-party services including but not limited to:

  • Google Android Management API
  • Android Device Policy
  • Managed Google Play
  • Firebase Cloud Messaging
  • Firebase Firestore or Realtime Database
  • Cloud hosting providers

These third-party services may process data according to their own privacy policies and terms.
